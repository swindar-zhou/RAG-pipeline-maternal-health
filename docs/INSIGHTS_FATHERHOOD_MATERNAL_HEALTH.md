# Fatherhood Programs & Maternal Health: Research Insights

## Why Fatherhood Programs Belong in a Maternal Health Taxonomy

Maternal health has traditionally been framed around the birthing person alone. However, a
growing body of evidence shows that **paternal involvement is a significant, modifiable
determinant of maternal health outcomes**. Programs that support fathers and partners during
the perinatal period directly improve mothers' well-being -- making them a legitimate and
important component of the maternal health ecosystem.

This document summarizes the evidence, describes the emerging program landscape, and explains
how the new "Partner & Family Engagement" taxonomy category connects to maternal outcomes.

---

## 1. Evidence Base: How Father Involvement Improves Maternal Health

### 1.1 Reduced Postpartum Depression

The most robust finding comes from a **systematic review and meta-analysis** published in the
*Journal of Epidemiology & Community Health* (Yargawa & Leonardi-Bee, 2015):

- Male involvement during **pregnancy** was associated with **64% reduced odds** of
  postpartum depression (OR = 0.36, 95% CI: 0.19-0.68)
- Male involvement during the **postpartum period** showed **66% reduced odds** of
  postpartum depression (OR = 0.34, 95% CI: 0.18-0.62)
- The protective effect was stronger when partners were involved across both periods,
  not just during delivery

This is particularly significant given that postpartum depression affects approximately
1 in 7 mothers and is a leading cause of maternal morbidity and mortality.

### 1.2 Improved Healthcare Utilization

Father/partner involvement is associated with:

- **Increased skilled birth attendance** -- women with involved partners are more likely
  to deliver with a skilled provider
- **Higher postnatal care uptake** -- partner support facilitates access to postpartum
  check-ups and follow-up care
- **Better prenatal care adherence** -- partners who understand the importance of prenatal
  visits can support attendance and transportation

### 1.3 Reduced Maternal Stress and Isolation

Programs that engage fathers address key risk factors for poor maternal outcomes:

- **Social isolation** -- a recognized risk factor for perinatal mood disorders. Partner
  engagement provides built-in social support
- **Decision-making burden** -- shared healthcare decision-making reduces maternal cognitive
  and emotional load
- **Economic stress** -- fatherhood programs that include employment/financial services
  reduce household economic instability, a major social determinant of maternal health

### 1.4 Co-Parenting Conflict Reduction

Research from the ACF's CHaRMED study and related evaluations shows:

- Healthy co-parenting education **reduces intimate partner violence**
- Improved communication skills lead to **better conflict resolution** during the high-stress
  perinatal period
- Reduced parental conflict is associated with **lower maternal cortisol levels** and better
  mental health outcomes

The mechanism operates through three parallel pathways:

1. **Direct support pathway**: Fathers who receive parenting education become active
   participants in prenatal visits, birth, and postpartum care -- reducing the burden
   on mothers and improving healthcare engagement.

2. **Economic pathway**: Employment services and financial literacy components of
   fatherhood programs reduce household economic instability -- a key social determinant
   of health that affects maternal stress and access to care.

3. **Relationship pathway**: Co-parenting and relationship education reduces conflict
   and intimate partner violence -- both of which are associated with adverse maternal
   and infant outcomes.

## 3. Program Landscape

### 3.1 Florida: Father First Initiative

**Scale**: $68.9 million appropriated by the Florida Legislature (HB 7065, 2022)

**Operator**: Florida Department of Children and Families (DCF), implemented by Family
First Inc.

**Grant Programs** ($21.2M announced):
- **Comprehensive Needs of Fathers Grant** -- employment assistance, child support
  management, parenting skills
- **Evidence-Based Education Programs for Fathers Grant** -- parent coaching, co-parenting
  collaboration, newborn/infant care
- **Mentorship Programs for At-Risk Male Students Grant** -- social, emotional, and
  cognitive skill development

**Website**: [fatherfirstfl.com](https://fatherfirstfl.com/)

**Relevance to maternal health**: The initiative explicitly targets fathers during the
perinatal period, with services that parallel and complement maternal health programs
(e.g., newborn care education, co-parenting support).

### 3.2 Federal: ACF Responsible Fatherhood Grants

The Administration for Children and Families (ACF), Office of Family Assistance, funds
Responsible Fatherhood (RF) programs nationwide. Programs must provide services in three
core areas:

1. **Parenting and fatherhood** -- parenting skills, child development knowledge
2. **Healthy marriage and relationships** -- communication, co-parenting, conflict resolution
3. **Economic stability** -- employment, financial literacy, career development

Key evaluation: The *Parents and Children Together* (PACT) study evaluated four RF programs
and found favorable impacts on co-parenting quality and economic outcomes.

### 3.3 California: County-Level Programs

Several California counties -- the focus of this project's data collection -- have
fatherhood components within their maternal health infrastructure:

- **Alameda County**: Maintains a dedicated [Fatherhood Initiative](https://acphd.org/fatherhood-initiative/)
  within the Public Health Department
- **Fresno County**: UCSF-funded evaluation of an adapted fatherhood curriculum to
  strengthen paternal involvement during pregnancy among Black men -- directly targeting
  preterm birth disparities
- **Statewide**: California's Perinatal Equity Initiative (PEI) serves "pregnant and
  parenting Black women **and their partners**" -- explicitly including fathers in a
  maternal health program

## 4. Implications for the Database

### 4.1 New Taxonomy Category

We added **"Partner & Family Engagement"** as the 14th program category, with 3 new
program types:

| Program Type | Description | SDOH Domain | Blueprint Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Responsible Fatherhood Programs | State/federal fatherhood initiatives | Social Support | Goal 5: Social |
| Co-Parenting Support Programs | Relationship & co-parenting education | Social Support | Goal 5: Social |
| Paternal Perinatal Support | Father/partner perinatal-specific programs | Social Support | Goal 5: Social |

### 4.2 What to Look for When Scraping County Websites

When extending the pipeline to discover fatherhood/partner programs on county health
department websites, look for:

- **Dedicated fatherhood pages** within health departments (e.g., Alameda County)
- **Partner inclusion language** in existing maternal programs (e.g., PEI serving "and
  their partners")
- **Co-parenting classes** listed under perinatal/family services
- **"Boot Camp for New Dads"** or similar perinatal father education programs
- **Links to state fatherhood initiatives** (e.g., Father First FL, state DCF pages)
- **Home visiting programs** that explicitly mention father engagement components

### 4.3 Cross-State Expansion Considerations

As the project expands beyond California, fatherhood programs become even more relevant:

- **Florida** has the largest state-level fatherhood investment ($68.9M)
- Most states have ACF-funded Responsible Fatherhood grantees
- Some states integrate fatherhood into Title V MCH programs
- Federal MIECHV home visiting programs increasingly include father engagement components
